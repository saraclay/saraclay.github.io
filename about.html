I started coding websites when I was 12 years old, making the most out of the text editor on Neopets.com. I thought bringing a webpage to life on a real, living site was an amazing thing.

When I got my first iPhone in college - an iPhone 4S to be exact - the same kind of fascination struck me. With just one tap, I had the ability to open up a whole universe of Tweets, videos, or just about anything.

After graduating college, I landed a job at Microsoft doing product marketing for open source - a one-of-a-kind role and experience that I'm truly grateful about. Yet, while in marketing, I was still developing websites and sketching out app ideas on the side. It got to the point where I realized that perhaps that my 12-year-old self was onto something. So I decided to take the leap and switch into UX.

Once I started my first role in UX, I found that my backgrounds in marketing, journalism, and web development complemented what I was working on, and I've felt like this is a discipline I've been practicing for much longer than I've realized. It's exciting to be part of the changing face and increasing prominence and importance of design in all industries.

Outside of work and side projects, I'm obsessed with finding the best hole-in-the-wall restaurants in Seattle as well as cooking Japanese food - I'm 99% sure that I'll be opening either a small bento shop or a Japanese bakery someday. I also enjoy running and boxing and currently sing alto in The Acoustix, a Microsoft a cappella group.
