MSW - Microsoft Employee Portal

Project
Redesigning and updating Microsoft's Employee portal

Role
UX and UI designer

Starting from (literally) day one, I was making hi-fi comps for MSW, Microsoft's internal employee portal. The homepage of the portal alone receives approximately 1 million pageviews per month. Because Microsoft's design language has been undergoing change, moving away from elements such as the blocks adapted from Windows 8, the current portal looked very outdated.

Because MSW is hosted on Sharepoint, there were some interaction components that we couldn't change. But for the most part, we were able to persuade stakeholders on the majority of our design changes, as well as going for a cleaner look and feel.

After I joined this project, we were able to double the amount of hi-fi comps we could show during our bi-weekly stakholder syncs. With this progress, we hit the project deadline with some time to spare.

The new portal went live in January 2017.

Learnings:
Another reason why we were able to move so quickly was because our stakeholders on the business and technical side were very flexible and open to hearing all of our ideas, which was quite nice. If I had been part of this project at an earlier stage, I would've like to create events for Optimizely and tagged the site more thoroughly for ClickTale on the current site.
